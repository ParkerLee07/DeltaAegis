# DeltaAegis v0.45 Telemetry Quality Policy

Status: Stage 0G policy correction.

## 1. Purpose

DeltaAegis evaluates telemetry quality separately from raw evidence. Imported
source artifacts remain immutable. The quality decision is a versioned,
auditable policy result that can change through review without rewriting the
original NetSniper bundle.

Quality answers two different questions:

1. Is this telemetry safe and authorized to retain?
2. Which operational effects may this telemetry produce?

A bundle can be safe enough to retain while still being blocked from active
state mutation.

## 2. Quality states

### ACCEPTED

The bundle passes integrity, authorization, supported-contract, and inventory
checks. Positive observations may update state normally.

Absence-based mutations and alert resolution are still coverage-gated. Accepted
does not mean that every host or collector permits negative evidence.

### DEGRADED

The bundle is safe and useful, but evidence coverage or provenance is
incomplete.

Degraded telemetry may add positive observations and positive-evidence alerts.
It may not:

- remove assets because they were not observed;
- resolve alerts from absence;
- independently create high-severity classification alerts; or
- replace a stronger prior classification with weaker evidence.

### QUARANTINED

The bundle is safely retainable, but trust is unresolved. It is stored in a
quarantine location and shown in the Telemetry Quality Center.

Quarantined telemetry cannot update assets, classifications, alerts, risk, or
current state before authorized review.

### REJECTED

The bundle is unsafe, unauthorized, unreadable, tampered with, or uses an
unrecognized contract.

DeltaAegis retains a rejection receipt and audit metadata only. Rejected
telemetry is not copied into trusted or quarantine evidence stores and cannot be
overridden into another state. A corrected bundle must be imported as a new
attempt.

## 3. Decision precedence

The most restrictive triggered state wins:

```text
REJECTED > QUARANTINED > DEGRADED > ACCEPTED
```

Informational acceptance reasons never cancel a more restrictive reason.

## 4. NetSniper compatibility

### NetSniper v2.1

A complete and valid v2.1 capability manifest and host-classification contract
starts as accepted. It is downgraded when partial collection, contradictions,
inventory loss, artifact problems, or policy violations are present.

### NetSniper v2.0

A valid v2.0 bundle is recognized but defaults to degraded because it cannot
prove the v2.1 capability, collector, evidence-provenance, and negative-evidence
contracts.

A v2.0 bundle may add positive observations. It may not drive disappearance,
absence-based resolution, or high-severity classification actions by default.

### Unknown future schemas

Unknown schemas fail closed as rejected until DeltaAegis explicitly supports
them. Version strings alone do not grant compatibility.

## 5. Operational effects

### Positive observations

Accepted and degraded bundles may add directly observed assets, services, and
findings. Degraded effects remain visibly marked with their quality state.

### Absence and removal

Asset removal, service closure, disappearance events, and alert resolution
require independently proven complete coverage. Quality-state promotion alone
never creates that proof.

### Classification replacement

Accepted telemetry may replace an existing classification only under the
confidence and contradiction policy.

Degraded telemetry may replace a classification only when the new evidence is
strictly stronger and creates no downgrade in evidence quality. Otherwise,
DeltaAegis creates a review event.

### Alerts and risk

Degraded telemetry may produce alerts from directly observed positive evidence.
It cannot independently create high-severity alerts from device classification
alone.

Risk derived from degraded evidence is uncertainty-capped and visibly marked.
Quarantined and rejected telemetry cannot change risk.

## 6. Reviews and overrides

- VIEWER may neither review nor override.
- ANALYST may review and annotate.
- ADMIN may review and override permitted states.
- Actor identity comes from the authenticated session.
- Caller-supplied actor fields are rejected or ignored.
- Every override requires a reason and durable audit evidence.
- The original automated state and reasons remain immutable.
- Rejected is non-overridable.
- An override never bypasses independent coverage requirements.

Allowed transitions are:

- ACCEPTED → QUARANTINED
- DEGRADED → ACCEPTED
- DEGRADED → QUARANTINED
- QUARANTINED → DEGRADED
- QUARANTINED → ACCEPTED

## 7. Required implementation invariants

1. Raw source evidence is never rewritten by quality evaluation.
2. Automated state and current reviewed state are stored separately.
3. Decision reasons use a controlled vocabulary.
4. The most restrictive reason determines automated state.
5. Rejected bundles retain receipt metadata only.
6. Quarantined bundles cannot mutate active state.
7. Degraded bundles cannot apply absence-based mutations.
8. v2.0 bundles default to degraded.
9. Unknown future schemas fail closed.
10. Manual overrides are reasoned, session-derived, and audited.
11. Promotion does not manufacture negative-evidence coverage.
12. Re-importing the same run ID with different content is rejected.
13. Bundle-relative paths remain confined.
14. Hash mismatch is never overridable.
15. Quality state is visible in APIs, reports, bundle drilldown, and the
    Telemetry Quality Center.

## 8. Contract examples

The contract includes examples for:

- a fully accepted v2.1 bundle;
- a degraded partial v2.1 bundle;
- a quarantined v2.1 bundle that reports host loss;
- a rejected bundle with an integrity mismatch; and
- a valid v2.0 bundle admitted under degraded compatibility.

These examples are policy fixtures. They do not implement the v0.45 runtime.

## 9. Stage 0G host-uncertainty and privilege correction

Stage 0G separates bundle telemetry sufficiency from host-classification
certainty and from the scanner process UID:

- `unprivileged_scan` is informational when every requested collector completed.
  Actual collector failure, unavailability, partial execution, incomplete
  inventory, or disabled negative evidence still degrades independently.
- `classification_review_present` and `classification_unknown_present` remain
  visible host-level reasons, but do not downgrade otherwise complete bundle
  telemetry. Accepted-state classification updates and high-severity
  classification alerts remain confidence-gated.
- `identity_collision` remains quarantining and fail-closed.
- Historical Stage 0F decisions remain immutable. New evaluations use
  `deltaaegis-v0.45-stage0g`.
